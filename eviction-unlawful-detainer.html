Eviction Process & Court Forms

Understanding the Eviction Process

California Courts Self-Help

The California Courts Self-Help website provides step-by-step guidance on the residential eviction process from both the tenant's and the landlord's perspective, including information about notice types, timelines, and what to expect at each stage.

  • Overview of the Residential Eviction Process in California Covers the process from both tenant's and landlord's perspectives.
  • How to Start or Respond to an Eviction Action Information about notice types and how to proceed as a landlord or tenant.

Eviction & Unlawful Detainer Forms

Court Forms & Filing

The forms needed for eviction and unlawful detainer actions are available from both the Santa Barbara County Superior Court and the statewide Judicial Council. You may also use the Odyssey Guide and File system to prepare and e-file certain documents.

  • SB Superior Court: Self-Help Evictions Page Local court resources and forms for eviction and unlawful detainer cases.
  • Judicial Council: Unlawful Detainer Forms Statewide court forms for unlawful detainer actions in California.
  • SB Superior Court: Evictions/Unlawful Detainers Division Clerk's office contact information and filing fees.

Odyssey Guide and File (E-Filing)

Online Filing Tool

Odyssey Guide and File is an interactive software program that helps you draft the appropriate legal documents through a series of interview-style questions. It is available only for certain types of actions and only for electronic filing. Do not use this tool if you prefer not to e-file. Scroll down the linked page to access the tool.

  • SB Superior Court: E-Filing (Odyssey Guide and File)
  • Odyssey Guide and File: User Guide (PDF)

Landlord-Tenant Laws

State of California

State Landlord-Tenant Laws

California has extensive laws governing the landlord-tenant relationship, including rules about security deposits, rent increases, habitability standards, eviction procedures, and more. The following resources can help you understand your rights and obligations under state law.

  • CA Attorney General: Landlord-Tenant Issues and Related Laws Official guidance from the California Department of Justice.
  • Overview of Landlord-Tenant Laws in California (Nolo Press) Nolo Press publishes books about the law written by attorneys for non-attorneys.
  • California Statutes: Read the Relevant Laws To find a specific section (for example, Civil Code section 1946.1), go to the "Code" box in the upper right corner, select "CIV" from the dropdown, and enter the section number.
  • 2019 Tenant Protection Act (AB 1482) Statewide rent cap and just-cause eviction protections for eligible residential tenancies.
