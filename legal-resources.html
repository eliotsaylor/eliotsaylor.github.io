Local Government

Municipal Codes & Local Ordinances

County of Santa Barbara & Cities

Municipal codes contain the local laws and ordinances that govern the County of Santa Barbara and each of its incorporated cities. These codes cover topics such as zoning, building regulations, business licensing, public safety, and more. Select a jurisdiction below to view its municipal code.

County of Santa Barbara City of Carpinteria City of Goleta City of Lompoc City of Santa Barbara City of Santa Maria City of Solvang Isla Vista (unincorporated)

Isla Vista is an unincorporated community located approximately 9 miles west of Santa Barbara. As an unincorporated area, it is governed by the County of Santa Barbara rather than its own city council.

State Government

California State Government

State of California

California's state government is organized into three branches: the Legislature (which creates laws), the Judiciary (which interprets laws), and the Executive (which enforces laws). The links below will help you find California statutes, court information, and other state government resources.

  • California State Government (ca.gov) Find information about state agencies, departments, and services including vital records such as birth, marriage, and death certificates.
  • California Legislative Information Search California statutes (state laws), review Assembly and Senate Bill information, and track pending legislation.
  • California State Senate
  • California State Assembly
  • California Judicial Branch (the Courts) The official website of the California Courts, including court forms, rules, and information about the state court system.
  • Find Your Superior Court There are 58 Superior Courts in California, one in each county. Locate your county's court here.
  • California Attorney General
  • State Bar of California Look up a licensed attorney, file a complaint, or find information about the legal profession in California.

Federal Government

Federal Government Resources

United States Government

The federal government of the United States is made up of three branches: the Legislative branch (Congress), the Executive branch (the President and federal agencies), and the Judicial branch (the federal courts). The links below provide access to federal laws, court information, and other government resources.

  • USA.gov The official web portal of the United States government with information about federal agencies, benefits, and services.
  • United States Supreme Court
  • Federal Courts (uscourts.gov) The home of the federal judiciary, including information on district courts, circuit courts, and the Supreme Court.
  • Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals Santa Barbara County is within the Ninth Circuit, which covers federal appeals from California and other western states.
  • United States Congress (congress.gov) Search federal legislation, the Congressional Record, and committee reports.
  • U.S. Senate
  • U.S. House of Representatives
  • Library of Congress Access the nation's library, including historical legal documents, legislative records, and research materials.

Tips for Legal Research

Start with the right jurisdiction. Legal issues may be governed by local, state, or federal law, or a combination. Knowing which jurisdiction applies to your situation will help you search in the right place.

Check for current versions. Laws change frequently. Always verify that the statute or ordinance you are reading is the most recent version. Government websites generally provide the current text.

Ask for help. Law Library staff can assist you with finding legal resources and locating relevant materials. We cannot provide legal advice, but we are happy to point you in the right direction.

Visit the Law Library. In addition to online resources, the Law Library maintains a collection of legal reference materials. You do not need a library card and everyone is welcome.
